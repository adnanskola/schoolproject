Changes in the Polar Zone due to Climate Change

In the last 35 years, the temperature at the poles has increased significantly. From 1971 to 2019, the temperature of the poles increased by 3.1 degrees Celsius. It hasn't increased that much in the past millions of years. Are we going to lose the Poles soon?

Bild Quelle: www.klimareporter.de

According to Dirk Notz, Director of the research group "Meereis im Erdsystem", Max-Planck-Institut, even before 2050 we will experience a summer in which the Northpole will have no ice. The Southpole isnt any better tho. On the 6th February 2020, record-high temperatures have been measured on the south pole, 18,3 Degrees Celsius (64,94 Degrees Fahrenheit), the Ice is melting 6 times faster than in 1980.
From 1992 to 2022, Antartica lost about 2.670 Billion tons of ice, that increased the sea level by 7,4 millimeters.

Is it too late to do something?

There are some changes which are impossible to revert, for example the loss of some shelf ice areas*, but there is MUCH which can be stopped or even reverted.

*shelf ice = Big ice area that is floating in the water with atleast 2m height.

Are all changes negative?

Although we would lose the poles through these changes, which is horrible, some people see the chance to make money. If there is no ice on the poles anymore it allows boats to drive there and get to their destination faster. It also allows us to get Oil which is under the water that is under the ice.
